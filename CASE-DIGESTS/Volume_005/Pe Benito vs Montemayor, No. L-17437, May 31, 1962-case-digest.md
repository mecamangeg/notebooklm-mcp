I. CAPTION
MENO PEBENITO v. PRESIDENT ZOSIMO MONTEMAYOR of the MINDANAO AGRICULTURAL COLLEGE, G.R. No. L-17437, May 31, 1962, Paredes, J.

II. FACTS
Meno Pe Benito, a temporary instructor at Mindanao Agricultural College, filed a petition for Mandamus and Prohibition against the College President, Zosimo Montemayor, to prevent his termination from service [1]. Montemayor filed an answer asserting his authority to dispense with temporary employees but appeared at the hearing without counsel [2], [3]. Unaware of legal procedure, Montemayor submitted the case on the pleadings without presenting evidence [3], [4]. The lower court ruled in favor of Pe Benito, ordering his reinstatement and salary payment [3].

The Solicitor General subsequently filed a petition for relief from judgment, arguing that Montemayorâ€™s failure to notify the Office of the Solicitor General and his act of appearing alone constituted mistake and excusable negligence, as the case involved official acts of government officials [5], [6]. The lower court denied the petition for relief, prompting this appeal [6].

III. ISSUE/S
W/N the petition for relief from judgment based on mistake and excusable negligence should be granted.

IV. RULING
**YES.** The Supreme Court held that the ends of justice would be better served by granting the relief. Montemayor was not a lawyer and was unfamiliar with the technicalities of procedure or the law; he could not have known the implications of submitting the case only on the pleadings [4]. His appearance without the benefit of counsel amounted to a deprivation of the chance to be heard, constituting manifest mistake and excusable negligence [4], [7]. Citing *Alonzo v. Villamor*, the Court emphasized that "[l]egal rights are too valuable to be risked, and should not be chanced on a toss of coins or dice" [7].

Accordingly, the order denying the motion for relief from judgment was set aside, and the case was remanded to the court of origin for a new trial [7].
