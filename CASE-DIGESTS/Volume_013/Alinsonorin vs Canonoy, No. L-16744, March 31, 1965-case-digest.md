I. CAPTION
SIMPLICIO ALINSONORIN v. MATEO M. CANONOY and ANDREA SALUD, G.R. No. L-16744, March 31, 1965, Dizon, J.

II. FACTS
Respondent Andrea Salud filed a complaint for separation of property against her husband, petitioner Simplicio Alinsonorin [1]. The trial court rendered a decision ordering Alinsonorin to pay Salud P20.00 monthly as her liquidated share of the produce of the conjugal properties [2]. This judgment became final and executory [3]. However, Alinsonorin failed to pay the allowance, prompting Salud to file a motion to declare him in contempt of court [2].

During the hearing, Alinsonorin claimed inability to pay because he had sold the conjugal properties to their grandson for P3,000.00 [2]. Resolving the motion for contempt, respondent Judge Canonoy issued an order on January 12, 1960, requiring Alinsonorin to deposit the P3,000.00 sale proceeds in court and to pay Saludâ€™s monthly pension from said deposit [4]. Alinsonorin filed a petition for certiorari, arguing that the judge lacked jurisdiction and that the order constituted an invalid modification of a final judgment [5].

III. ISSUE/S
W/N the trial court had jurisdiction to order the deposit of sale proceeds and payment of pension therefrom in resolving a motion for contempt.

IV. RULING
**YES.** The Supreme Court ruled that the respondent judge had the authority to issue the contested order [3]. Since the judgment requiring the monthly payment of P20.00 had become final, it was the judge's duty to ensure it was faithfully carried out [3]. The Court found that Alinsonorin sold the conjugal properties specifically to avoid complying with his duty under the judgment [3]. Consequently, it was within the judge's authority to order the deposit of the proceeds and require their use to pay the amount due to the wife [3]. The Court held that granting this relief in a contempt proceeding was proper, as it served as a means to compel compliance with the final judgment [3].

The petition was dismissed and the writ denied [6].
