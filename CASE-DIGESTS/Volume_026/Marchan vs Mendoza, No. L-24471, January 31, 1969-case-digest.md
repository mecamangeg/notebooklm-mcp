I. CAPTION
SILVERIO MARCHAN and PHILIPPINE RABBIT BUS CO., INC. v. ARSENIO MENDOZA, LEONARDA ILAYA, and ZENAIDA MENDOZA, G.R. No. L-24471, January 31, 1969, 26 SCRA 89, Fernando, J.

II. FACTS
Petitioners Silverio Marchan and Philippine Rabbit Bus Co., Inc. filed a Motion for Reconsideration regarding the Supreme Courtâ€™s decision promulgated on August 30, 1968. They specifically questioned the portion of the decision condemning them to pay P30,000.00 in exemplary damages, P5,000.00 in attorney's fees, and interest [1]. The Court of Appeals previously held the petitioners liable for exemplary damages, a ruling sustained by the Supreme Court [2]. In their motion, petitioners argued that exemplary damages were improper, citing *Rotea v. Halili* (claiming no participation by the employer in the wrongful act) and *Munsayac v. De Lara* (claiming the act must be coetaneous with the breach) [3], [4]. The underlying facts established that the petitioner's driver was found guilty of gross negligence and recklessness which caused injury to the respondents [5].

III. ISSUE/S
W/N the award of exemplary damages to the respondents is proper.

IV. RULING
**YES.** The Court denied the Motion for Reconsideration. Under **Article 2232 of the Civil Code**, in contracts and quasi-contracts, the court may award exemplary damages if the defendant acted in a "wanton, fraudulent, **reckless**, oppressive or malevolent manner" [5].

The Court held that the petitioners' reliance on *Rotea* and *Munsayac* was misplaced. *Rotea* involved subsidiary liability where no aggravating circumstances were present, while *Munsayac* involved acts subsequent to the breach of contract. Instead, the Court applied the ruling in *Laguna-Tayabas Bus Co. v. Diasanta*, establishing that where a driver (agent) is found guilty of **recklessness** (gross negligence), the award of exemplary damages against the carrier is justified under Article 2232 [5]. Consequently, since the award of exemplary damages is proper, the grant of attorney's fees is also justifiable [6].

**Motion for reconsideration denied.**
