I. CAPTION
CHAMBER OF TAXICAB SERVICES, INC., ET AL. v. PUBLIC SERVICE COMMISSION, ET AL., G.R. Nos. L-22785, L-22826 and L-22937, January 31, 1966, Dizon, J.

II. FACTS
Petitioners filed a petition for certiorari challenging the validity of a provisional permit issued on March 5, 1964. On September 25, 1964, respondent Silva filed a motion to dismiss the petition, informing the Court that the provisional permit in question had been canceled. Petitioners subsequently filed a statement on October 26, 1964, interposing no objection to the immediate dismissal of the cases.

III. ISSUE/S
W/N the petition should be dismissed for being moot.

IV. RULING
**YES.** The Court held that the cancellation of the provisional permit, which was the subject matter of the petition, rendered the case moot [1, 2]. With the petitioners interposing no objection to the motion, the Court granted the motion to dismiss and ordered the cases dismissed [2, 3].
