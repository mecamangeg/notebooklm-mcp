**I. CAPTION**
IN THE MATTER OF THE PETITION OF SUSANO SY TO BE ADMITTED A CITIZEN OF THE PHILIPPINES. SUSANO SY v. REPUBLIC OF THE PHILIPPINES, G.R. No. L-19581, April 29, 1966, Bengzon, C.J.

**II. FACTS**
Susano Sy filed a petition for naturalization, which was granted by the Court of First Instance of Negros Oriental [1, 2]. The Republic of the Philippines appealed the decision, maintaining that the petitioner did not possess all the qualifications required by law [2]. The records revealed that for the years preceding the petition (1958, 1959, 1960, and 1961), Syâ€™s income was P3,044, P3,328, P2,065, and P3,600, respectively, resulting in an average annual income of only P3,000.00 [2, 3].

**III. ISSUE/S**
W/N the petitioner has a lucrative trade or occupation to qualify for naturalization.

**IV. RULING**
**NO.** The Supreme Court ruled that an average annual income of P3,000.00 is not considered a "lucrative" trade or occupation required for admission to Philippine citizenship [4, 5]. The Court relied on recent rulings in *Uy vs. Republic* (L-20208 and L-20799), which established that an applicant with an income of P3,600 per annum or P250.00 per month does not possess a lucrative income [5].

Consequently, the decision of the lower court was **REVERSED** and the petitioner's application for naturalization was **DENIED** [5].
