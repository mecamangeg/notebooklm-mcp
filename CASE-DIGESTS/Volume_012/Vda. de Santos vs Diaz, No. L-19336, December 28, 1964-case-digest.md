I. CAPTION
JOSEFA VDA. DE SANTOS, ET AL. v. ANDRES J. DIAZ, G.R. No. L-19336, December 28, 1964, Concepcion, J.

II. FACTS
On March 6, 1928, Mamerto Santos filed an application for registration of several parcels of land in Las Pi√±as under Act No. 496. This was dismissed in 1929 due to his failure to amend a survey plan [1]. Years later, his heirs (petitioners) filed a second application for the same lands, which was dismissed in 1959 for "insufficiency of evidence" [1].

On August 4, 1960, petitioners filed the present application for registration [1]. Respondent Andres J. Diaz opposed the application regarding three lots, claiming fee simple ownership [2]. The Court of First Instance of Rizal dismissed the application on the grounds of *res judicata* and estoppel, citing the previous dismissals [3]. Petitioners appealed to the Supreme Court [3].

III. ISSUE/S
W/N the orders of dismissal in the two previous registration cases bar the present proceedings under the principle of *res judicata*.

IV. RULING
**NO.** The Supreme Court held that the previous dismissals did not constitute *res judicata*.

Under Act No. 496, even as amended by Act No. 3621, a dismissal of a registration application does not necessarily bar a subsequent application unless there was a contentious issue essential to the application of *res judicata* [4]. In the previous cases, no opposition was filed; the first was dismissed for failure to submit a plan and the second for insufficiency of evidence [4]. Thus, there was no adjudication of conflicting interests or determination of ownership [4].

The Court distinguished this from proceedings under the Public Land Law or Cadastral Act, where a dismissal declares the land part of the public domain and binds the whole world [5]. Citing *Henson v. Director of Lands*, the Court reiterated that the purpose of the Land Registration Act is to encourage owners to register their titles; barring re-application when defects are cured or new evidence is found would impair the usefulness of the system [6, 7].

The order appealed from is **SET ASIDE** and the case is **REMANDED** to the lower court for further proceedings [8].

V. CONCURRING/DISSENTING OPINIONS
None. (Bengzon, C.J., Bautista Angelo, Reyes, J.B.L., Barrera, Regala, Makalintal, Bengzon, J.P., and Zaldivar, JJ., concurred) [8].
