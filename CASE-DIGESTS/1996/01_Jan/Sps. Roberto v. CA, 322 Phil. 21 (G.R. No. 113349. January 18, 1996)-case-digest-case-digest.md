II. FACTS
Petitioners Spouses Mondonedo filed a complaint against private respondent Security Bank and Trust Company in the RTC of Makati. During the pre-trial hearing, the RTC dismissed the complaint after petitioners' counsel arrived late, allegedly due to a flooded street caused by a typhoon. The RTC subsequently denied their motion for reconsideration. Instead of filing an ordinary appeal, petitioners filed a petition for *certiorari* with the Court of Appeals (CA) to set aside the RTC orders. The CA dismissed the petition, ruling that the proper remedy was appeal, not *certiorari*, and further noted that petitioners failed to state the date of receipt of the order denying their motion for reconsideration, rendering the timeliness of the petition indeterminable [2].

III. ISSUE/S
W/N *certiorari* is the proper remedy for a plaintiff declared non-suited for failure to appear at the pre-trial hearing [3].

IV. RULING
**NO.** The Supreme Court affirmed the CA, ruling that the proper remedy for a plaintiff declared non-suited is an **appeal**, not *certiorari* [3]. A dismissal for failure to appear at the pre-trial hearing is deemed an adjudication on the merits unless the order states otherwise [3]. Since the dismissal acts as a final resolution of the case, the correct recourse is to appeal the order of dismissal or the order denying the motion for reconsideration [3]. The Court reiterated the settled rule that where appeal is the proper remedy, *certiorari* will not lie [3]. The petition was denied and the temporary restraining order was lifted [3].
